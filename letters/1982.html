BERKSHIRE HATHAWAY INC.
BERKSHIRE HATHAWAY INC.
To the Stockholders of Berkshire Hathaway Inc.:
给伯克希尔哈撒韦公司的股东:
Operating earnings of $31.5 million in 1982 amounted to only 9.8% of beginning equity capital (valuing securities at cost), down from 15.2% in 1981 and far below our recent high of 19.4% in 1978. This decline largely resulted from:
1982年的营业利润为3,150万美元，仅占初始股本（按成本估值证券）的9.8%，低于1981年的15.2%，远低于1978年的19.4%。 这种下降主要是由于:
(1) a significant deterioration in insurance underwriting results;
（一）保险承保结果显着恶化;
(2) a considerable expansion of equity capital without a corresponding growth in the businesses we operate directly; and
(2)股权资本的大幅扩张，而我们直接经营的业务却没有相应的增长；及
(3) a continually-enlarging commitment of our resources to investment in partially-owned, nonoperated businesses; accounting rules dictate that a major part of our pro-rata share of earnings from such businesses must be excluded from Berkshires reported earnings.
(3)我们的资源不断扩大，用于投资部分拥有的非经营性企业；会计规则规定，我们从这些企业中按比例分配的大部分收入必须排除在伯克希尔公司报告的收入之外。
It was only a few years ago that we told you that the operating earnings/equity capital percentage, with proper allowance for a few other variables, was the most important yardstick of single-year managerial performance. While we still believe this to be the case with the vast majority of companies, we believe its utility in our own case has greatly diminished. You should be suspicious of such an assertion. Yardsticks seldom are discarded while yielding favorable readings. But when results deteriorate, most managers favor disposition of the yardstick rather than disposition of the manager.
就在几年前，我们才告诉大家，经营收益/股本百分比，加上适当的其他一些变量，是衡量单年管理业绩的最重要标准。 虽然我们仍然认为绝大多数公司都是如此，但我们认为它在我们自己的情况下的效用已经大大减少。 你应该怀疑这样的断言。 准绳很少被丢弃，同时产生有利的读数。 但是，当结果恶化时，大多数管理者倾向于使用衡量标准，而不是使用管理者。
To managers faced with such deterioration, a more flexible measurement system often suggests itself: just shoot the arrow of business performance into a blank canvas and then carefully draw the bullseye around the implanted arrow. We generally believe in pre-set, long-lived and small bullseyes. However, because of the importance of item (3) above, further explained in the following section, we believe our abandonment of the operating earnings/equity capital bullseye to be warranted.
对于面临这种恶化的管理者来说，更灵活的测量系统往往会自我暗示：只需将业务绩效的箭头射入空白画布，然后在植入的箭头周围仔细绘制靶心。 我们一般相信预先设定的，长寿的和小的牛眼。 然而，由于上文第(3)项的重要性，我们认为放弃经营收益/股本牛眼是有必要的。
Non-Reported Ownership Earnings
非报告所有权收益
The appended financial statements reflect accounting earnings that generally include our proportionate share of earnings from any underlying business in which our ownership is at least 20%. Below the 20% ownership figure, however, only our share of dividends paid by the underlying business units is included in our accounting numbers; undistributed earnings of such less-than-20%-owned businesses are totally ignored.
所附的财务报表反映的会计收益通常包括我们从任何基础业务的收益中所占的比例，而我们的所有权至少为20%。 然而，低于20%的所有权数字，我们的会计数字中只包括我们由基础业务部门支付的股息份额；这些低于20%的企业的未分配收益完全被忽略。
There are a few exceptions to this rule; e.g., we own about 35% of GEICO Corporation but, because we have assigned our voting rights, the company is treated for accounting purposes as a less- than-20% holding. Thus, dividends received from GEICO in 1982 of $3.5 million after tax are the only item included in our accountingearnings. An additional $23 million that represents our share of GEICOs undistributed operating earnings for 1982 is totally excluded from our reported operating earnings. If GEICO had earned less money in 1982 but had paid an additional $1 million in dividends, our reported earnings would have been larger despite the poorer business results. Conversely, if GEICO had earned an additional $100 million - and retained it all - our reported earnings would have been unchanged. Clearly accounting earnings can seriously misrepresent economic reality.
这条规则有一些例外；例如，我们拥有GEICO公司约35%的股份，但由于我们已经分配了投票权，因此出于会计目的，该公司被视为少于20%的股份。 因此，1982年从GEICO获得的税后350万美元的股息是我们会计收入中唯一的项目。 另外2 300万美元是我们在GEICOs1982年未分配经营收益中所占的份额,完全不包括在我们报告的经营收益中。 如果GEICO在1982年赚的钱少了，但又支付了100万美元的股息，那么尽管业务业绩较差，我们报告的收益会更大。 相反，如果GEICO获得了额外的1亿美元-并保留了这一切-我们报告的收益将保持不变。 显然，会计收益会严重歪曲经济现实。
We prefer a concept of economic earnings that includes all undistributed earnings, regardless of ownership percentage. In our view, the value to all owners of the retained earnings of a business enterprise is determined by the effectiveness with which those earnings are used - and not by the size of ones ownership percentage. If you have owned .01 of 1% of Berkshire during the past decade, you have benefited economically in full measure from your share of our retained earnings, no matter what your accounting system. Proportionately, you have done just as well as if you had owned the magic 20%. But if you have owned 100% of a great many capital-intensive businesses during the decade, retained earnings that were credited fully and with painstaking precision to you under standard accounting methods have resulted in minor or zero economic value. This is not a criticism of accounting procedures. We would not like to have the job of designing a better system. Its simply to say that managers and investors alike must understand that accounting numbers are the beginning, not the end, of business valuation.
我们更喜欢一个经济收益的概念，包括所有未分配收益，无论所有权百分比如何。 我们认为，企业留存收益对所有所有者的价值取决于这些收益的使用效率，而不是所有权百分比的大小。 如果你拥有。在过去的十年里，无论你的会计制度如何，你从我们的留存收益中获得了充分的经济利益。 按比例，你做得和你拥有魔法20%一样好。 但是，如果你在这十年里拥有了大量资本密集型企业的100%，那么根据标准会计方法全额贷记并非常精确地贷记给你的留存收益就会导致经济价值微乎其微或为零。 这不是对会计程序的批评。 我们不希望有设计一个更好的系统的工作。 简单来说，经理人和投资者都必须明白会计数字是企业估值的开始，而不是结束。
In most corporations, less-than-20% ownership positions are unimportant (perhaps, in part, because they prevent maximization of cherished reported earnings) and the distinction between accounting and economic results we have just discussed matters little. But in our own case, such positions are of very large and growing importance. Their magnitude, we believe, is what makes our reported operating earnings figure of limited significance.
在大多数公司中，低于20％的所有权头寸并不重要（也许部分原因是它们阻止了宝贵报告收益的最大化），以及我们刚刚讨论过的会计和经济结果之间的分 但在我们自己的情况下，这种立场是非常大的，越来越重要。 我们认为，它们的规模使我们报告的经营收益数字意义有限。
In our 1981 annual report we predicted that our share of undistributed earnings from four of our major non-controlled holdings would aggregate over $35 million in 1982. With no change in our holdings of three of these companies - GEICO, General Foods and The Washington Post - and a considerable increase in our ownership of the fourth, R. J. Reynolds Industries, our share of undistributed 1982 operating earnings of this group came to well over $40 million. This number - not reflected at all in our earnings - is greater than our total reported earnings, which include only the $14 million in dividends received from these companies. And, of course, we have a number of smaller ownership interests that, in aggregate, had substantial additional undistributed earnings.
我们在1981年年报中预测，1982年，我们从四个主要非控股持股中所占的未分配收益总额将超过3,500万美元。 由于我们对其中三家公司（GEICO，General Foods和The Washington Post）的持股没有变化，而我们对第四家公司R.J.Reynolds Industries的所有权也大幅增加，我们在该集团1982年未分配的经营收益中所占的份额远远超过了4,000万美元。 这一数字--根本没有反映在我们的收益中--大于我们报告的总收入，其中只包括从这些公司获得的1400万美元的股息。 当然，我们还有一些较小的所有权权益，总体而言，这些权益有大量额外的未分配收益。
We attach real significance to the general magnitude of these numbers, but we dont believe they should be carried to ten decimal places. Realization by Berkshire of such retained earnings through improved market valuations is subject to very substantial, but indeterminate, taxation. And while retained earnings over the years, and in the aggregate, have translated into at least equal market value for shareholders, the translation has been both extraordinarily uneven among companies and irregular and unpredictable in timing.
我们重视这些数字的总体大小，但我们不认为它们应该被带到小数点后十位。 伯克希尔通过改善市场估值实现这些留存收益需要缴纳非常可观但不确定的税收。 虽然多年来的留存收益（总体而言）已经转化为股东至少相等的市场价值，但公司之间的转换非常不均衡，时间也不规则和不可预测。
However, this very unevenness and irregularity offers advantages to the value-oriented purchaser of fractional portions of businesses. This investor may select from almost the entire array of major American corporations, including many far superior to virtually any of the businesses that could be bought in their entirety in a negotiated deal. And fractional-interest purchases can be made in an auction market where prices are set by participants with behavior patterns that sometimes resemble those of an army of manic-depressive lemmings.
然而，这种非常不均匀和不规则性为部分业务的价值导向购买者提供了优势。 这个投资者可以从几乎所有的美国大公司中进行选择，其中包括许多远远优于几乎任何可以通过谈判全部收购的企业。 部分利息购买可以在拍卖市场中进行，在拍卖市场中，价格由参与者设定，其行为模式有时类似于狂躁抑郁的旅鼠的行为模式。
Within this gigantic auction arena, it is our job to select businesses with economic characteristics allowing each dollar of retained earnings to be translated eventually into at least a dollar of market value. Despite a lot of mistakes, we have so far achieved this goal. In doing so, we have been greatly assisted by Arthur Okuns patron saint for economists - St. Offset. In some cases, that is, retained earnings attributable to our ownership position have had insignificant or even negative impact on market value, while in other major positions a dollar retained by an investee corporation has been translated into two or more dollars of market value. To date, our corporate over- achievers have more than offset the laggards. If we can continue this record, it will validate our efforts to maximize economic earnings, regardless of the impact upon accounting earnings.
在这个巨大的拍卖舞台上，我们的工作是选择具有经济特征的企业，让每一美元的留存收益最终转化为至少一美元的市场价值。 尽管有很多错误，但到目前为止我们已经实现了这一目标。 在这样做的过程中，我们得到了arthur Okuns经济学家守护神-St.Offset的极大帮助。 在某些情况下，即归因于我们的所有权状况的留存收益对市场价值产生了微不足道甚至负面的影响，而在其他主要头寸中，被投资公司保留的一美元已转化为两美元或两美元以上的市场价值。 到目前为止，我们公司的过度成功者已经超过了落后者。 如果我们能够继续保持这一记录，它将验证我们最大化经济收益的努力，而不管对会计收益的影响如何。
Satisfactory as our partial-ownership approach has been, what really makes us dance is the purchase of 100% of good businesses at reasonable prices. Weve accomplished this feat a few times (and expect to do so again), but it is an extraordinarily difficult job - far more difficult than the purchase at attractive prices of fractional interests.
令人满意的是，我们的部分所有权方法一直是，真正让我们跳舞的是以合理的价格购买100％的好企业。 我们已经完成了这一壮举几次（并期望再次这样做），但这是一项非常困难的工作-远比以有吸引力的部分利益价格购买困难得多。
As we look at the major acquisitions that others made during 1982, our reaction is not envy, but relief that we were non- participants. For in many of these acquisitions, managerial intellect wilted in competition with managerial adrenaline The thrill of the chase blinded the pursuers to the consequences of the catch. Pascals observation seems apt: It has struck me that all mens misfortunes spring from the single cause that they are unable to stay quietly in one room.
当我们看到其他人在1982年进行的重大收购时，我们的反应不是嫉妒，而是宽慰我们不是参与者。 因为在许多收购中，管理智力在与管理肾上腺素的竞争中萎靡不振，追逐的刺激使追赶者对捕获的后果视而不见。 帕斯卡尔的观察似乎很恰当：让我吃惊的是，所有男人的不幸都是因为他们不能静静地呆在一个房间里。
(Your Chairman left the room once too often last year and almost starred in the Acquisition Follies of 1982. In retrospect, our major accomplishment of the year was that a very large purchase to which we had firmly committed was unable to be completed for reasons totally beyond our control. Had it come off, this transaction would have consumed extraordinary amounts of time and energy, all for a most uncertain payoff. If we were to introduce graphics to this report, illustrating favorable business developments of the past year, two blank pages depicting this blown deal would be the appropriate centerfold.)
（你的主席去年经常离开房间一次，几乎出演了1982年的收购愚蠢。 回想起来，我们今年的主要成就是，我们坚定承诺的一笔非常大的购买由于完全超出我们控制范围的原因而无法完成。 如果成功，这笔交易将消耗大量的时间和精力，所有这些都是最不确定的回报。 如果我们在这份报告中引入图形，说明过去一年有利的业务发展，两页空白页描绘这笔被吹走的交易将是适当的插页。)
Our partial-ownership approach can be continued soundly only as long as portions of attractive businesses can be acquired at attractive prices. We need a moderately-priced stock market to assist us in this endeavor. The market, like the Lord, helps those who help themselves. But, unlike the Lord, the market does not forgive those who know not what they do. For the investor, a too-high purchase price for the stock of an excellent company can undo the effects of a subsequent decade of favorable business developments.
我们的部分所有权方法只有在部分有吸引力的企业能够以有吸引力的价格收购的情况下才能继续健全。 我们需要一个价格适中的股票市场来帮助我们进行这项努力。 市场，像主一样，帮助那些帮助自己的人。 但是，与上帝不同，市场不会原谅那些不知道自己做什么的人。 对于投资者来说，一个优秀公司的股票过高的购买价格可以消除随后十年有利的业务发展的影响。
Should the stock market advance to considerably higher levels, our ability to utilize capital effectively in partial- ownership positions will be reduced or eliminated. This will happen periodically: just ten years ago, at the height of the two-tier market mania (with high-return-on-equity businesses bid to the sky by institutional investors), Berkshires insurance subsidiaries owned only $18 million in market value of equities, excluding their interest in Blue Chip Stamps. At that time, such equity holdings amounted to about 15% of our insurance company investments versus the present 80%. There were as many good businesses around in 1972 as in 1982, but the prices the stock market placed upon those businesses in 1972 looked absurd. While high stock prices in the future would make our performance look good temporarily, they would hurt our long-term business prospects rather than help them. We currently are seeing early traces of this problem.
如果股市升至相当高的水平，我们在部分所有权头寸中有效利用资本的能力将被降低或消除。 这种情况会周期性地发生：就在十年前，在两层市场狂热的高峰期（高回报率的股票业务由机构投资者竞标天空），伯克希尔保险子公司只拥有1800万美元的股票市值，不包括他们对蓝筹印花的兴趣。 当时，这些股权持有量约占我们保险公司投资的15%，而目前为80%。 1972年的好企业和1982年一样多，但1972年股票市场对这些企业的价格看起来很荒谬。 虽然未来的高股价会使我们的业绩暂时看起来不错，但它们会损害我们的长期业务前景，而不是帮助他们。 我们目前看到这个问题的早期痕迹。
Long-Term Corporate Performance
长期企业表现
Our gain in net worth during 1982, valuing equities held by our insurance subsidiaries at market value (less capital gain taxes payable if unrealized gains were actually realized) amounted to $208 million. On a beginning net worth base of $519 million, the percentage gain was 40%.
我们在1982年的净资产收益，按市场价值计算我们保险子公司持有的股票（如果实际实现未实现的收益，则应扣除资本利得税）达2.08亿美元。 在5.19亿美元的初始净值基础上，百分比收益为40％。
During the 18-year tenure of present management, book value has grown from $19.46 per share to $737.43 per share, or 22.0% compounded annually. You can be certain that this percentage will diminish in the future. Geometric progressions eventually forge their own anchors.
在现任管理层的18年任期内，账面价值从每股19.46美元增长到每股737.43美元，即每年复合22.0%。 你可以肯定，这一比例将在未来减少。 几何级数最终形成了自己的锚。
Berkshires economic goal remains to produce a long-term rate of return well above the return achieved by the average large American corporation. Our willingness to purchase either partial or total ownership positions in favorably-situated businesses, coupled with reasonable discipline about the prices we are willing to pay, should give us a good chance of achieving our goal.
Berkshires的经济目标仍然是产生远高于平均大型美国公司所实现的回报的长期回报率。 我们愿意购买位于有利位置的企业的部分或全部所有权头寸，再加上我们愿意支付的价格合理的纪律，应该给我们一个很好的机会实现我们的目标。
Again this year the gain in market valuation of partially- owned businesses outpaced the gain in underlying economic value of those businesses. For example, $79 million of our $208 million gain is attributable to an increased market price for GEICO. This company continues to do exceptionally well, and we are more impressed than ever by the strength of GEICOs basic business idea and by the management skills of Jack Byrne. (Although not found in the catechism of the better business schools, Let Jack Do It works fine as a corporate creed for us.)
今年，部分拥有的企业的市场估值涨幅再次超过了这些企业的基本经济价值涨幅。 例如，我们2.08亿美元的收益中有7,900万美元是由于GEICO的市场价格上涨。 这家公司继续做得非常好，我们比以往任何时候都对GEICOs基本商业理念的力量和Jack Byrne的管理技能印象深刻。 （虽然在更好的商学院的教义问答中没有找到，但让杰克做它对我们来说是一个很好的企业信条。)
However, GEICOs increase in market value during the past two years has been considerably greater than the gain in its intrinsic business value, impressive as the latter has been. We expected such a favorable variation at some point, as the perception of investors converged with business reality. And we look forward to substantial future gains in underlying business value accompanied by irregular, but eventually full, market recognition of such gains.
然而，GEICOs在过去两年中的市场价值增长远远大于其内在商业价值的增长，后者令人印象深刻。 我们预计在某个时候会出现这样一个有利的变化，因为投资者的看法与商业现实相融合。 我们期待着潜在商业价值的巨大未来收益，伴随着不规则但最终完全的市场对这些收益的认可。
Year-to-year variances, however, cannot consistently be in our favor. Even if our partially-owned businesses continue to perform well in an economic sense, there will be years when they perform poorly in the market. At such times our net worth could shrink significantly. We will not be distressed by such a shrinkage; if the businesses continue to look attractive and we have cash available, we simply will add to our holdings at even more favorable prices.
然而，每年的差异不可能始终对我们有利。 即使我们的部分拥有的业务在经济意义上继续表现良好，也会有多年在市场上表现不佳。 在这种时候，我们的净资产可能会大幅缩水。 我们不会为这样的萎缩而苦恼；如果业务继续看起来很有吸引力，而且我们有现金可用，我们只会以更优惠的价格增加我们的资产。

You should be further aware that the 1982 combined ratio of 109.5 represents a best case estimate. In a given year, it is possible for an insurer to show almost any profit number it wishes, particularly if it (1) writes long-tail business (coverage where current costs can be only estimated, because claim payments are long delayed), (2) has been adequately reserved in the past, or (3) is growing very rapidly. In insurance, as elsewhere, the reaction of weak managements to weak operations is often weak accounting. (Its difficult for an empty sack to stand upright.)

The great majority of managements, however, try to play it straight. But even managements of integrity may subconsciously be less willing in poor profit years to fully recognize adverse loss trends. Industry statistics indicate some deterioration in loss reserving practices during 1982 and the true combined ratio is likely to be modestly worse than indicated by our table.
然而，绝大多数管理层都试图直截了当。 但即使是诚信的管理层也可能下意识地不太愿意在盈利不佳的年份充分认识到不利的损失趋势。 工业统计数字表明,1982年损失保留做法有些恶化,实际的综合比率可能略低于我们的表所示。
The conventional wisdom is that 1983 or 1984 will see the worst of underwriting experience and then, as in the past, the cycle will move, significantly and steadily, toward better results. We disagree because of a pronounced change in the competitive environment, hard to see for many years but now quite visible.
传统的智慧是，1983年或1984年将看到最糟糕的承保经验，然后，像过去一样，周期将显着而稳定地走向更好的结果。 我们不同意，因为竞争环境发生了明显的变化，多年来很难看到，但现在相当明显。
To understand the change, we need to look at some major factors that affect levels of corporate profitability generally. Businesses in industries with both substantial over-capacity and a commodity product (undifferentiated in any customer-important way by factors such as performance, appearance, service support, etc.) are prime candidates for profit troubles. These may be escaped, true, if prices or costs are administered in some manner and thereby insulated at least partially from normal market forces. This administration can be carried out (a) legally through government intervention (until recently, this category included pricing for truckers and deposit costs for financial institutions), (b) illegally through collusion, or (c) extra- legally through OPEC-style foreign cartelization (with tag-along benefits for domestic non-cartel operators).
为了理解这一变化，我们需要研究一些影响公司盈利水平的主要因素。 在具有大量产能过剩和商品产品的行业中的企业（通过性能、外观、服务支持等因素以任何客户重要的方式未分化）。）是利润问题的主要候选人。 如果价格或成本以某种方式管理，从而至少部分地与正常的市场力量隔离，这些可能会被逃脱。 这一管理可以(a)通过政府干预合法地进行(直到最近,这一类包括卡车司机的定价和金融机构的存款费用),(b)通过勾结非法进行,或(c)通过欧佩克式的外国卡特尔法外进行(对国内非卡特尔经营者有附带利益)。
If, however, costs and prices are determined by full-bore competition, there is more than ample capacity, and the buyer cares little about whose product or distribution services he uses, industry economics are almost certain to be unexciting. They may well be disastrous.
然而，如果成本和价格是由全面竞争决定的，那么有足够的能力，而买方很少关心他使用谁的产品或分销服务，那么行业经济学几乎肯定是不合理的。 他们很可能是灾难性的。
Hence the constant struggle of every vendor to establish and emphasize special qualities of product or service. This works with candy bars (customers buy by brand name, not by asking for a two-ounce candy bar) but doesnt work with sugar (how often do you hear, Ill have a cup of coffee with cream and C & H sugar, please).
因此，每个供应商不断努力建立和强调产品或服务的特殊品质。 这适用于糖果棒（客户按品牌购买，而不是要求两盎司的糖果棒），但不适用于糖（你多久听到一次，我会喝一杯加奶油和c＆h糖的咖啡）。
In many industries, differentiation simply cant be made meaningful. A few producers in such industries may consistently do well if they have a cost advantage that is both wide and sustainable. By definition such exceptions are few, and, in many industries, are non-existent. For the great majority of companies selling commodityproducts, a depressing equation of business economics prevails: persistent over-capacity without administered prices (or costs) equals poor profitability.
在许多行业中，差异化根本不可能变得有意义。 如果这些行业的一些生产商具有广泛和可持续的成本优势，它们可能会一直做得很好。 根据定义，这种例外很少，并且在许多行业中不存在。 对于绝大多数销售商品的公司来说，一个令人沮丧的商业经济学等式占上风：没有管理价格（或成本）的持续产能过剩等于盈利能力差。
Of course, over-capacity may eventually self-correct, either as capacity shrinks or demand expands. Unfortunately for the participants, such corrections often are long delayed. When they finally occur, the rebound to prosperity frequently produces a pervasive enthusiasm for expansion that, within a few years, again creates over-capacity and a new profitless environment. In other words, nothing fails like success.
当然，随着容量缩小或需求扩大，超容量最终可能会自我纠正。 不幸的是，对于参与者来说，这种更正往往被长期拖延。 当它们最终出现时，繁荣的反弹往往会产生普遍的扩张热情，在几年内，这种热情再次创造了产能过剩和新的无利可图的环境。 换句话说，没有什么比成功更失败的了。
What finally determines levels of long-term profitability in such industries is the ratio of supply-tight to supply-ample years. Frequently that ratio is dismal. (It seems as if the most recent supply-tight period in our textile business - it occurred some years back - lasted the better part of a morning.)
最终决定这些行业长期盈利水平的是供应紧张与供应充足的年份的比率。 这一比例通常是令人沮丧的。 （好像我们纺织业最近的供应紧张时期--发生在几年前--持续了一个上午的大部分时间。)
In some industries, however, capacity-tight conditions can last a long time. Sometimes actual growth in demand will outrun forecasted growth for an extended period. In other cases, adding capacity requires very long lead times because complicated manufacturing facilities must be planned and built.
然而，在某些行业，产能紧张的情况可能会持续很长时间。 有时，实际需求增长将超过预期增长一段时间。 在其他情况下，增加产能需要很长的交货时间，因为必须规划和建造复杂的制造设施。
But in the insurance business, to return to that subject, capacity can be instantly created by capital plus an underwriters willingness to sign his name. (Even capital is less important in a world in which state-sponsored guaranty funds protect many policyholders against insurer insolvency.) Under almost all conditions except that of fear for survival - produced, perhaps, by a stock market debacle or a truly major natural disaster - the insurance industry operates under the competitive sword of substantial overcapacity. Generally, also, despite heroic attempts to do otherwise, the industry sells a relatively undifferentiated commodity-type product. (Many insureds, including the managers of large businesses, do not even know the names of their insurers.) Insurance, therefore, would seem to be a textbook case of an industry usually faced with the deadly combination of excess capacity and a commodity product.
但在保险业务，回到这个主题，能力可以立即由资本加上承销商愿意签署他的名字。 （在一个由国家担保的担保基金保护许多投保人免受保险公司破产的世界里，即使资本也不那么重要。 在几乎所有条件下，除了对生存的恐惧--也许是股市崩盘或真正重大的自然灾害--保险业都是在巨大产能过剩的竞争剑下运作的。 一般来说，尽管有英勇的尝试，但该行业销售的商品类型产品相对未分化。 （许多保险公司，包括大型企业的经理，甚至不知道他们的保险公司的名字。 因此，保险似乎是一个行业的教科书案例，通常面临着产能过剩和商品产品的致命组合。
Why, then, was underwriting, despite the existence of cycles, generally profitable over many decades? (From 1950 through 1970, the industry combined ratio averaged 99.0. allowing all investment income plus 1% of premiums to flow through to profits.) The answer lies primarily in the historic methods of regulation and distribution. For much of this century, a large portion of the industry worked, in effect, within a legal quasi-administered pricing system fostered by insurance regulators. While price competition existed, it was not pervasive among the larger companies. The main competition was for agents, who were courted via various non-price-related strategies.
那么，为什么尽管存在周期，但承销在几十年内通常是有利可图的？ （从1950年到1970年，工业综合比率平均为99.0。 允许所有投资收入加上1%的保费流向利润. 答案主要在于监管和分配的历史方法。 在本世纪的大部分时间里，该行业的很大一部分实际上是在保险监管机构培育的合法准管理定价体系中工作的。 虽然价格竞争存在，但在大公司中并不普遍。 主要的竞争是代理商，他们通过各种非价格相关的策略来追求。
For the giants of the industry, most rates were set through negotiations between industry bureaus (or through companies acting in accord with their recommendations) and state regulators. Dignified haggling occurred, but it was between company and regulator rather than between company and customer. When the dust settled, Giant A charged the same price as Giant B - and both companies and agents were prohibited by law from cutting such filed rates.
对于行业巨头来说，大多数费率是通过行业局（或通过按照其建议行事的公司）和州监管机构之间的谈判确定的。 有尊严的讨价还价发生了，但它是在公司和监管机构之间，而不是在公司和客户之间。 当尘埃落定时，巨人A收取与巨人B相同的价格-法律禁止公司和代理商削减此类税率。
The company-state negotiated prices included specific profit allowances and, when loss data indicated that current prices were unprofitable, both company managements and state regulators expected that they would act together to correct the situation. Thus, most of the pricing actions of the giants of the industry were gentlemanly, predictable, and profit-producing. Of prime importance - and in contrast to the way most of the business world operated - insurance companies could legally price their way to profitability even in the face of substantial over- capacity.
公司与国家谈判的价格包括具体的利润津贴，当损失数据表明当前价格无利可图时，公司管理层和国家监管机构都希望他们共同采取行动纠正这种情况。 因此，该行业巨头的定价行为大多是温和的，可预测的，并产生利润。 最重要的是--与大多数商业世界的运作方式相反--保险公司即使面对巨大的产能过剩，也可以合法地为盈利定价。
That day is gone. Although parts of the old structure remain, far more than enough new capacity exists outside of that structure to force all parties, old and new, to respond. The new capacity uses various methods of distribution and is not reluctant to use price as a prime competitive weapon. Indeed, it relishes that use. In the process, customers have learned that insurance is no longer a one-price business. They wont forget.
那一天已经过去了。 虽然旧结构的某些部分仍然存在,但在该结构之外存在着足够多的新能力,迫使新旧各方作出反应。 新的能力使用各种分配方法，并且不愿意将价格作为主要的竞争武器。 事实上，它喜欢这种使用。 在这个过程中，客户已经了解到保险不再是一个价格单一的业务。 他们不会忘记的。
Future profitability of the industry will be determined by current competitive characteristics, not past ones. Many managers have been slow to recognize this. Its not only generals that prefer to fight the last war. Most business and investment analysis also comes from the rear-view mirror. It seems clear to us, however, that only one condition will allow the insurance industry to achieve significantly improved underwriting results. That is the same condition that will allow better results for the aluminum, copper, or corn producer - a major narrowing of the gap between demand and supply.
该行业未来的盈利能力将取决于当前的竞争特征，而不是过去的竞争特征。 许多管理者迟迟没有认识到这一点。 它不仅是喜欢打最后一场战争的将军。 大多数商业和投资分析也来自后视镜。 然而，我们似乎很清楚，只有一个条件可以让保险业取得显着改善的承保结果。 同样的条件也将为铝、铜或玉米生产商带来更好的结果--这是供需差距的主要缩小。
Unfortunately, there can be no surge in demand for insurance policies comparable to one that might produce a market tightness in copper or aluminum. Rather, the supply of available insurance coverage must be curtailed. Supply, in this context, is mental rather than physical: plants or companies need not be shut; only the willingness of underwriters to sign their names need be curtailed.
不幸的是，对保险政策的需求不会激增，这可能会导致铜或铝的市场紧缩。 相反，必须减少可用保险的供应。 在这种情况下，供应是精神上的而不是身体上的：工厂或公司不需要关闭;只有承销商签署他们的名字的意愿需要减少。
This contraction will not happen because of generally poor profit levels. Bad profits produce much hand-wringing and finger-pointing. But they do not lead major sources of insurance capacity to turn their backs on very large chunks of business, thereby sacrificing market share and industry significance.
这种收缩不会发生，因为一般较差的利润水平。 不良的利润产生了很多令人费解和指责。 但它们并没有导致主要的保险能力来源背弃非常大的业务，从而牺牲市场份额和行业意义。
Instead, major capacity withdrawals require a shock factor such as a natural or financial megadisaster. One might occur tomorrow - or many years from now. The insurance business - even taking investment income into account - will not be particularly profitable in the meantime.
相反，主要的产能撤出需要一个冲击因素，如自然或金融巨头。 一个可能发生在明天-或许多年后。 保险业务-即使考虑到投资收入-在此期间也不会特别有利可图。
When supply ultimately contracts, large amounts of business will be available for the few with large capital capacity, a willingness to commit it, and an in-place distribution system. We would expect great opportunities for our insurance subsidiaries at such a time.
当供应最终合同时，大量的业务将可用于少数具有大资本能力，愿意承诺的业务和就地分销系统。 我们期待我们的保险子公司在这样的时候有很大的机会。
During 1982, our insurance underwriting deteriorated far more than did the industrys. From a profit position well above average, we, slipped to a performance modestly below average. The biggest swing was in National Indemnitys traditional coverages. Lines that have been highly profitable for us in the past are now priced at levels that guarantee underwriting losses. In 1983 we expect our insurance group to record an average performance in an industry in which average is very poor.
在1982年，我们的保险承保恶化的程度远远超过了行业。 从远高于平均水平的利润位置，我们的表现略低于平均水平。 最大的波动是国家赔偿的传统保险. 过去对我们来说高利润的线路现在定价在保证承销损失的水平上。 在1983年，我们预计我们的保险集团将在一个平均水平非常差的行业中录得平均业绩。
Two of our stars, Milt Thornton at Cypress and Floyd Taylor at Kansas Fire and Casualty, continued their outstanding records of producing an underwriting profit every year since joining us. Both Milt and Floyd simply are incapable of being average. They maintain a passionately proprietary attitude toward their operations and have developed a business culture centered upon unusual cost-consciousness and customer service. It shows on their scorecards.
我们的两位明星，赛普拉斯的米尔特*桑顿（Milt Thornton）和堪萨斯州火灾与伤亡的弗洛伊德*泰勒（Floyd Taylor），自从加入我们以来，他们每年都保持着出色的承销利润记录。 米尔特和弗洛伊德都无法做到平均水平. 他们对自己的经营保持着热情的专有态度，并形成了以不寻常的成本意识和客户服务为中心的商业文化。 它显示在他们的记分卡上。
During 1982, parent company responsibility for most of our insurance operations was given to Mike Goldberg. Planning, recruitment, and monitoring all have shown significant improvement since Mike replaced me in this role.
在1982年，我们大部分保险业务的母公司责任交给了迈克*戈德伯格。 自从迈克取代我担任这个角色以来，计划、招聘和监控都有显着的改善。
GEICO continues to be managed with a zeal for efficiency and value to the customer that virtually guarantees unusual success. Jack Byrne and Bill Snyder are achieving the most elusive of human goals - keeping things simple and remembering what you set out to do. In Lou Simpson, additionally, GEICO has the best investment manager in the property-casualty business. We are happy with every aspect of this operation. GEICO is a magnificent illustration of the high-profit exception we described earlier in discussing commodity industries with over- capacity - a company with a wide and sustainable cost advantage. Our 35% interest in GEICO represents about $250 million of premium volume, an amount considerably greater than all of the direct volume we produce.
GEICO继续以对客户效率和价值的热情进行管理，这几乎保证了非同寻常的成功。 杰克*伯恩（Jack Byrne）和比尔*斯奈德（Bill Snyder）正在实现人类最难以捉摸的目标-保持简单并记住你打算做的事情。 此外，在Lou Simpson，GEICO拥有财产损失业务中最好的投资经理。 我们对这次行动的每一个方面都很满意。 GEICO是我们之前在讨论产能过剩的商品行业时所描述的高利润例外的一个宏伟例证-这是一家具有广泛和可持续成本优势的公司我们在GEICO的35%权益代表了大约2.5亿美元的保费量，这一数额大大超过了我们生产的所有直接数量。
Issuance of Equity
发行股本
Berkshire and Blue Chip are considering merger in 1983. If it takes place, it will involve an exchange of stock based upon an identical valuation method applied to both companies. The one other significant issuance of shares by Berkshire or its affiliated companies that occurred during present managements tenure was in the 1978 merger of Berkshire with Diversified Retailing Company.
伯克希尔和蓝筹印花正在考虑在1983年合并。 如果发生，它将涉及基于适用于两家公司的相同估值方法的股票交换。 伯克希尔或其附属公司在目前的管理任期内发行的另一个重要股票是1978年伯克希尔与多元化零售公司的合并。
Our share issuances follow a simple basic rule: we will not issue shares unless we receive as much intrinsic business value as we give. Such a policy might seem axiomatic. Why, you might ask, would anyone issue dollar bills in exchange for fifty-cent pieces? Unfortunately, many corporate managers have been willing to do just that.
我们的股票发行遵循一个简单的基本规则：除非我们获得尽可能多的内在商业价值，否则我们不会发行股票。 这样的政策似乎是公理的。 你可能会问，为什么有人会发行美元钞票来换取50美分的钞票？ 不幸的是，许多公司经理都愿意这样做。
The first choice of these managers in making acquisitions may be to use cash or debt. But frequently the CEOs cravings outpace cash and credit resources (certainly mine always have). Frequently, also, these cravings occur when his own stock is selling far below intrinsic business value. This state of affairs produces a moment of truth. At that point, as Yogi Berra has said, You can observe a lot just by watching. For shareholders then will find which objective the management truly prefers - expansion of domain or maintenance of owners wealth.
这些经理在进行收购时的首选可能是使用现金或债务。 但Ceo们的渴望常常超过现金和信贷资源（当然我的总是如此）。 此外，当他自己的股票销售远远低于内在商业价值时，经常会出现这些渴望。 这种状况产生了一个真心话大冒险的时刻. 在这一点上，正如Yogi Berra所说，你可以通过观察来观察很多。 对于股东来说，他们会发现管理层真正喜欢哪个目标--扩大领域或维护所有者的财富。
The need to choose between these objectives occurs for some simple reasons. Companies often sell in the stock market below their intrinsic business value. But when a company wishes to sell out completely, in a negotiated transaction, it inevitably wants to - and usually can - receive full business value in whatever kind of currency the value is to be delivered. If cash is to be used in payment, the sellers calculation of value received couldnt be easier. If stock of the buyer is to be the currency, the sellers calculation is still relatively easy: just figure the market value in cash of what is to be received in stock.
出于一些简单的原因，需要在这些目标之间进行选择。 公司在股票市场上的销售往往低于其内在的商业价值。 但是，当一家公司希望在谈判交易中完全抛售时，它不可避免地希望-并且通常可以-以任何类型的货币获得全部业务价值。 如果要用现金付款，卖方计算收到的价值就不容易了。 如果买方的股票是货币，卖方的计算仍然相对容易：只需计算将收到的股票的现金市场价值。
Meanwhile, the buyer wishing to use his own stock as currency for the purchase has no problems if the stock is selling in the market at full intrinsic value.
同时，如果股票在市场上以全部内在价值出售，希望使用自己的股票作为购买货币的买家就没有问题。
But suppose it is selling at only half intrinsic value. In that case, the buyer is faced with the unhappy prospect of using a substantially undervalued currency to make its purchase.
但假设它仅以一半的内在价值出售。 在这种情况下，买方面临着使用大幅低估的货币进行购买的不愉快前景。
Ironically, were the buyer to instead be a seller of its entire business, it too could negotiate for, and probably get, full intrinsic business value. But when the buyer makes a partial sale of itself - and that is what the issuance of shares to make an acquisition amounts to - it can customarily get no higher value set on its shares than the market chooses to grant it.
具有讽刺意味的是，如果买方成为其整个业务的卖方，它也可以谈判并可能获得完整的内在商业价值。 但是，当买方进行部分出售时--这也是发行股票进行收购的结果--它通常不会获得比市场选择授予它更高的股票价值。
The acquirer who nevertheless barges ahead ends up using an undervalued (market value) currency to pay for a fully valued (negotiated value) property. In effect, the acquirer must give up $2 of value to receive $1 of value. Under such circumstances, a marvelous business purchased at a fair sales price becomes a terrible buy. For gold valued as gold cannot be purchased intelligently through the utilization of gold - or even silver - valued as lead.
然而，讨价还价的收购者最终使用被低估的（市场价值）货币来支付完全价值（谈判价值）的财产。 实际上，收购方必须放弃2美元的价值才能获得1美元的价值。 在这种情况下，以公平的销售价格购买的奇妙业务变成了可怕的购买。 因为作为黄金价值的黄金不能通过利用黄金-甚至银-作为铅价值明智地购买。
If, however, the thirst for size and action is strong enough, the acquirers manager will find ample rationalizations for such a value-destroying issuance of stock. Friendly investment bankers will reassure him as to the soundness of his actions. (Dont ask the barber whether you need a haircut.)
然而，如果对规模和行动的渴望足够强烈，收购方经理就会为这种破坏价值的股票发行找到充分的理由。 友好的投资银行家会让他放心，他的行动是正确的。 （不要问理发师是否需要理发。)
A few favorite rationalizations employed by stock-issuing managements follow:
股票发行管理层最喜欢的一些合理化措施如下:
(a) The company were buying is going to be worth a lot more in the future. (Presumably so is the interest in the old business that is being traded away; future prospects are implicit in the business valuation process. If 2X is issued for X, the imbalance still exists when both parts double in business value.)
(a)这家收购的公司将来会有更多的价值。 （据推测，对正在交易的旧业务的兴趣也是如此;未来前景隐含在业务估值过程中。 如果为X发行2X，则当两部分业务价值都翻倍时，不平衡仍然存在。)
(b) We have to grow. (Who, it might be asked, is the we? For present shareholders, the reality is that all existing businesses shrink when shares are issued. Were Berkshire to issue shares tomorrow for an acquisition, Berkshire would own everything that it now owns plus the new business, but your interest in such hard-to-match businesses as Sees Candy Shops, National Indemnity, etc. would automatically be reduced. If (1) your family owns a 120-acre farm and (2) you invite a neighbor with 60 acres of comparable land to merge his farm into an equal partnership - with you to be managing partner, then (3) your managerial domain will have grown to 180 acres but you will have permanently shrunk by 25% your familys ownership interest in both acreage and crops. Managers who want to expand their domain at the expense of owners might better consider a career in government.)
(b)我们必须成长。 （谁，它可能会被问到，是我们？ 对于目前的股东来说，现实是所有现有业务在股票发行时都会萎缩。 如果伯克希尔明天发行股票进行收购，伯克希尔将拥有它现在拥有的所有东西以及新的业务，但是你对糖果店，国家赔偿等难以匹配的业务的兴趣。 会自动减少。 如果（1）你的家庭拥有一个120英亩的农场，（2）你邀请一个拥有60英亩可比土地的邻居将他的农场合并成一个平等的伙伴关系--与你成为管理合伙人，那么（3）你的管理领域将增长到180英亩，但你将永久减少25%你的家庭对种植面积和作物的所有权。 想要以牺牲所有者为代价扩展其领域的经理可能更好地考虑在政府中工作。)
(c) Our stock is undervalued and weve minimized its use in this deal - but we need to give the selling shareholders 51% in stock and 49% in cash so that certain of those shareholders can get the tax-free exchange they want. (This argument acknowledges that it is beneficial to the acquirer to hold down the issuance of shares, and we like that. But if it hurts the old owners to utilize shares on a 100% basis, it very likely hurts on a 51% basis. After all, a man is not charmed if a spaniel defaces his lawn, just because its a spaniel and not a St. Bernard. And the wishes of sellers cant be the determinant of the best interests of the buyer - what would happen if, heaven forbid, the seller insisted that as a condition of merger the CEO of the acquirer be replaced?)
（c）我们的股票被低估，我们在这笔交易中最大限度地减少了它的使用-但我们需要给出售股东51％的股票和49％的现金，以便某些股东可以获得他们想要的 （这一论点承认，按住股票发行对收购方有利，我们喜欢这一点。 但是，如果以100％的基础利用股票对老业主造成伤害，那么很可能会以51％的基础伤害。 毕竟，如果一只猎犬玷污了他的草坪，一个人就不会被迷住，仅仅因为它是一只猎犬而不是一只圣伯纳德。 卖方的意愿不能成为买方最大利益的决定因素--如果卖方坚持以收购方的首席执行官为合并条件，会发生什么？)
There are three ways to avoid destruction of value for old owners when shares are issued for acquisitions. One is to have a true business-value-for-business-value merger, such as the Berkshire-Blue Chip combination is intended to be. Such a merger attempts to be fair to shareholders of both parties, with each receiving just as much as it gives in terms of intrinsic business value. The Dart Industries-Kraft and Nabisco Standard Brands mergers appeared to be of this type, but they are the exceptions. Its not that acquirers wish to avoid such deals; its just that they are very hard to do.
在为收购发行股票时，有三种方法可以避免旧所有者的价值破坏。 一个是有一个真正的商业价值为商业价值的合并，如伯克希尔-蓝筹印花组合的目的是。 这样的合并试图对双方的股东公平，每个股东在内在商业价值方面都能获得同样多的收益。 Dart Industries-Kraft和Nabisco Standard Brands的合并似乎属于这种类型，但它们是例外。 这不是收购者希望避免这样的交易，只是他们很难做到。
The second route presents itself when the acquirers stock sells at or above its intrinsic business value. In that situation, the use of stock as currency actually may enhance the wealth of the acquiring companys owners. Many mergers were accomplished on this basis in the 1965-69 period. The results were the converse of most of the activity since 1970: the shareholders of the acquired company received very inflated currency (frequently pumped up by dubious accounting and promotional techniques) and were the losers of wealth through such transactions.
第二条路线出现在收购方股票以或高于其内在商业价值的价格出售时。 在这种情况下，使用股票作为货币实际上可能会增加收购公司所有者的财富。 在1965-69时期，许多合并都是在此基础上完成的。 结果与1970年以来的大部分活动相反：被收购公司的股东获得了非常膨胀的货币（经常被可疑的会计和促销技术所吸引），并且通过这种交易成为财富的输家。
During recent years the second solution has been available to very few large companies. The exceptions have primarily been those companies in glamorous or promotional businesses to which the market temporarily attaches valuations at or above intrinsic business valuation.
近年来，第二种解决方案已经提供给很少的大公司例外情况主要是那些从事迷人或促销业务的公司，市场暂时将估值置于内在业务估值或高于内在业务估值。
The third solution is for the acquirer to go ahead with the acquisition, but then subsequently repurchase a quantity of shares equal to the number issued in the merger. In this manner, what originally was a stock-for-stock merger can be converted, effectively, into a cash-for-stock acquisition. Repurchases of this kind are damage-repair moves. Regular readers will correctly guess that we much prefer repurchases that directly enhance the wealth of owners instead of repurchases that merely repair previous damage. Scoring touchdowns is more exhilarating than recovering ones fumbles. But, when a fumble has occurred, recovery is important and we heartily recommend damage-repair repurchases that turn a bad stock deal into a fair cash deal.
第三种解决方案是收购方继续进行收购，但随后回购与合并中发行数量相等的股份。 通过这种方式，最初的股票换股票合并可以有效地转换为现金换股票收购。 这种回购是损坏修复动作。 普通读者会正确地猜测，我们更喜欢直接提升所有者财富的回购，而不是仅仅修复先前损害的回购。 得分触地得分比恢复触地得分更令人兴奋。 但是，当一个摸索已经发生，恢复是重要的，我们衷心建议损坏修复回购，把一个坏的股票交易变成一个公平的现金交易。
The language utilized in mergers tends to confuse the issues and encourage irrational actions by managers. For example, dilution is usually carefully calculated on a pro forma basis for both book value and current earnings per share. Particular emphasis is given to the latter item. When that calculation is negative (dilutive) from the acquiring companys standpoint, a justifying explanation will be made (internally, if not elsewhere) that the lines will cross favorably at some point in the future. (While deals often fail in practice, they never fail in projections - if the CEO is visibly panting over a prospective acquisition, subordinates and consultants will supply the requisite projections to rationalize any price.) Should the calculation produce numbers that are immediately positive - that is, anti-dilutive - for the acquirer, no comment is thought to be necessary.
合并中使用的语言往往会混淆问题，并鼓励管理者采取非理性的行动。 例如，稀释通常是在形式基础上仔细计算账面价值和当前每股收益。 特别强调后一项。 从收购公司的角度来看，当这种计算是负面的（稀释的）时，就会做出一个合理的解释（内部，如果不是其他地方的话），即这些线将在未来的某个时刻有利地交叉。 （虽然交易在实践中经常失败，但在预测中却永远不会失败-如果首席执行官明显地对未来的收购感到喘不过气来，下属和顾问将提供必要的预测来理顺任何价格。 如果计算得出的数字对收购方来说立即是正数--即反稀释性--则不认为有必要发表评论。
The attention given this form of dilution is overdone: current earnings per share (or even earnings per share of the next few years) are an important variable in most business valuations, but far from all powerful.
给予这种稀释形式的关注是过度的：当前每股收益（甚至未来几年的每股收益）是大多数业务估值的重要变量，但远非所有强大。
There have been plenty of mergers, non-dilutive in this limited sense, that were instantly value destroying for the acquirer. What really counts is whether a merger is dilutive or anti-dilutive in terms of intrinsic business value (a judgment involving consideration of many variables). A second language problem relates to the equation of exchange. If Company A announces that it will issue shares to merge with Company B, the process is customarily described as Company A to Acquire Company B, or B Sells to A. Clearer thinking about the matter would result if a more awkward but more accurate description were used: Part of A sold to acquire B, or Owners of B to receive part of A in exchange for their properties. In a trade, what you are giving is just as important as what you are getting. This remains true even when the final tally on what is being given is delayed. Subsequent sales of common stock or convertible issues, either to complete the financing for a deal or to restore balance sheet strength, must be fully counted in evaluating the fundamental mathematics of the original acquisition. (If corporate pregnancy is going to be the consequence of corporate mating, the time to face that fact is before the moment of ecstasy.)
第二语言问题涉及交换方程。 如果A公司宣布将发行股票与B公司合并，则通常将该过程描述为A公司收购B公司，或B向A出售。如果使用更尴尬但更准确的描述，则会更清楚地思考这个问题：a的一部分出售给b公司，或B的所有者获得A的一部分以换取他们的财产。 在交易中，你所给予的和你所获得的一样重要。 即使最后的计票被推迟，这仍然是正确的。 普通股或可转换发行的后续销售，无论是为了完成交易的融资还是为了恢复资产负债表实力，都必须在评估原始收购的基本数学时充分计算在内。 （如果公司怀孕将是公司交配的结果，那么面对这一事实的时间是在狂喜的时刻之前。)

在注册投资公司的情况下，给予与获得因素最容易计算。 假设投资公司X以资产价值的50％出售，希望与投资公司Y合并。假设X公司因此决定发行市值等于Ys资产价值100％的股票。

Finally, a word should be said about the double whammy effect upon owners of the acquiring company when value-diluting stock issuances occur. Under such circumstances, the first blow is the loss of intrinsic business value that occurs through the merger itself. The second is the downward revision in market valuation that, quite rationally, is given to that now-diluted business value. For current and prospective owners understandably will not pay as much for assets lodged in the hands of a management that has a record of wealth-destruction through unintelligent share issuances as they will pay for assets entrusted to a management with precisely equal operating talents, but a known distaste for anti-owner actions.
